What is excluded from the ROI — and why
The headline ROI reflects direct analyst labor savings only — the most concrete and universally comparable measure. Additional value your team may see includes:

① Faster IC turnaround — dossiers in hours rather than days, enabling quicker decisions.
② Opportunity cost of delayed IC decisions — real but not generically quantifiable.
③ Data licensing costs — included in PRIE fee, not double-counted.
④ Compliance or legal review costs — vary by jurisdiction and firm structure.
⑤ Team retention effects — directionally positive for analyst satisfaction; not quantified.

The ROI shown covers analyst labor savings on the selected tasks only — additional value drivers are presented separately in the Risk & Strategic Value tab.
Methodology & assumptions
ROI formula

ROI = Net savings ÷ Total PRIE cost × 100, where Total PRIE cost = PRIE-assisted labor cost + $30,000 annual fee. This is the standard investment ROI formula. The denominator is the total cost of the PRIE solution, not just the subscription fee in isolation.

Salary & hourly rate

Hourly rate = annual compensation ÷ 2,080 hrs (BLS standard: 52 wks × 40 hrs/wk). Bands from BLS OEWS May 2024 [4,5] and CFA Institute 2024 Compensation Study [6]. Band midpoints used in calculations.

Task scoping

Tasks are classified by whether they produce a shared deliverable (IC memo, benchmarking report — counted once per portfolio, not per analyst) or are performed independently by each analyst (filing reviews, surveillance, news monitoring). This prevents double-counting headcount.

Capacity ceiling

An 80% utilisation cap (1,664 hrs/analyst/yr) is applied. If selected tasks imply hours above this cap, all task hours are scaled proportionally. This prevents implausible output at high portfolio volumes.

PRIE-assisted hours

Remaining human time: filings 45 min, IC dossier 1.5 hrs (team total), benchmarking 45 min (team total), surveillance 20 min, news digest 15 min/day. All include analyst review, validation, and sign-off.

Benchmark sourcing

Where published ranges exist, this calculator uses the lower bound (e.g., ODD/IDD: 3–5 hrs → 4 hrs; IC memo: 8–15 hrs → 8 hrs; news: 1.6–1.8 hrs/day → 1.5 hrs/day). Each figure links directly to its source.

Scope note: This model estimates analyst labor cost savings on selected manual research tasks. Risk avoidance value, faster decision-making benefits, and expanded monitoring capacity are shown separately in the Risk & Strategic Value tab and are not included in the headline ROI figure.
